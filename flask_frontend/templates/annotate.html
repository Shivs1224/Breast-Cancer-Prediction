      Breast imaging notes

      Screening mammography is the primary tool for early breast cancer detection. Regular screening is associated with lower mortality; follow local guidelines for start age and interval.

      Findings may include masses (irregular shapes raise concern), calcifications (segmental or linear patterns merit workup), asymmetry, or architectural distortion.

      BI-RADS (overview) categories summarize management: incomplete (0), negative (1), benign (2), probably benign (3, short-interval follow-up), suspicious (4, biopsy considered), highly suggestive of malignancy (5), known biopsy-proven cancer (6).

      This tool helps outline regions of interest. It does not diagnose disease. All interpretations belong to a qualified radiologist.
